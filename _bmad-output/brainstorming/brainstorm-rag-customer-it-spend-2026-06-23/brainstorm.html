Technique 01 — Creative
🪨
First Principles Thinking

Stripped every assumption to bedrock. Started with "We need a RAG app" — and found the real problem underneath it.

The app isn't a budget lookup tool. It's a service-fit intelligence platform. The question isn't "how big is their budget?" — it's "which of our three services could this company buy right now?"
IT budgets are never published directly. They must be inferred from signals: public financials, job postings, LinkedIn headcount, tech stack tools, industry benchmarks.
Two jobs emerged: Discovery (breadth) — find targets; Pre-engagement Intel (depth) — know a company cold. Same data. Different lenses.
Three service lines, three signal sets: fixed-price work (capex/tech initiatives), staff augmentation (open reqs, attrition), coaching/team leading (rapid growth, tech debt).

Technique 04 — Structured
💼
Job to Be Done

Stripped the feature list and asked: what progress is a consultant trying to make in their life when they reach for this app?

Usage context: sitting at a desk — either planning before a known meeting, or looking for companies worth meeting. Not panicking. Planning. This is a research workspace, not a panic button.
Job 1 — Prospect smarter: Empty pipeline → qualified targets. Functional: filter by budget signals. Emotional: momentum. Social: pipeline activity.
Job 2 — Prep for a meeting: Uncertainty → confidence and credibility. Functional: know the company cold. Emotional: feel prepared. Social: look sharp in the room.
The two jobs connect: prospect → target → "prep for first call" handoff. Implies a lightweight "my companies" saved list. The app earns loyalty by following the consultant's workflow, not interrupting it.
Job hierarchy: Consultant (priority 1) → Prove AI capability (priority 2) → Impress demo audience (polish, not design driver)

The Three Hidden Connections
What the techniques revealed that wasn't said out loud
Connection 01 — The Root and the Demo Are the Same Argument
The Five Whys revealed the real goal: build AI credibility for the firm to stay competitive. The "We've done this before" feature, built with AI tools in 4 weeks, is that proof — not a side effect of it. The app and the capability story are the same thing. Every architecture choice, every week of velocity, every clean API is part of the argument.
Connection 02 — Fake Data Is the Secret Weapon
Mock data looks like a limitation. It's actually full control. Because you control every company profile, every demo answer is crafted to perfectly answer all 10 test questions. Real data at week 4 would be messier, less predictable, and harder to demo. The polished mock-data demo will be more impressive than a real-data version — and real data becomes a feature flag, not a prerequisite.
Connection 03 — The Standalone Constraint Solves the Integration Problem
"No external dependencies for v1" felt like a simplification for speed. But API-first standalone architecture is the correct design for a system that will eventually plug into something else. The constraint didn't compromise the architecture — it produced it. When integration day comes, the other system calls the same FastAPI endpoints the React UI already uses.
