# IT Spending Trends by Industry — 2025 Benchmark Report

**Domain:** industry-content
**Source:** Composite of Gartner, IDC, and Forrester benchmarks
**Date:** Q1 2025

## Overview

Enterprise IT spending as a percentage of revenue varies significantly by industry. Understanding these benchmarks helps consulting firms identify companies that are over- or under-investing relative to peers — both signals create opportunity.

## IT Spend as a Percentage of Revenue by Sector

| Industry | Low | Average | High | Key Driver |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Financial Services / Banking | 6.0% | 7.5% | 10%+ | Regulatory compliance, digital banking |
| Healthcare | 3.0% | 4.5% | 6.5% | EHR, interoperability mandates |
| Manufacturing | 1.5% | 2.8% | 4.5% | Operational technology, ERP modernization |
| Retail | 1.5% | 2.5% | 4.0% | E-commerce, omnichannel fulfillment |
| Logistics / Supply Chain | 2.5% | 3.5% | 5.0% | Visibility platforms, route optimization |
| Professional Services | 4.0% | 6.0% | 9.0% | Talent management, client delivery tools |

## Software Development Spend as a Percentage of Total IT Budget

On average, companies allocate 28–45% of their IT budget to software development (custom development, SaaS integration, and application maintenance). Companies undergoing digital transformation typically sit at 40–50% while operationally stable companies sit at 25–35%.

Key factors that increase the software development share:
- Active migration off legacy platforms (ERP, core banking, EHR)
- E-commerce or digital product buildout
- Regulatory compliance deadlines (FHIR, DORA, SOX automation)
- Competitive pressure from digital-native competitors

## Year-over-Year IT Budget Growth Patterns

Average enterprise IT budget growth in 2024 was 8.4% across all sectors, compared to 5.1% in 2023. Sectors with the highest growth:
1. **Retail** — 18–35% for companies in e-commerce expansion mode
2. **Financial Services** — 12–18% driven by digital banking and regulatory investment
3. **Healthcare** — 10–15% driven by interoperability mandates
4. **Manufacturing** — 8–12% driven by Industry 4.0 and ERP modernization

A company growing its IT budget at more than 1.5x the sector average is typically in active transformation — a strong signal for consulting engagement.

## Signals That Indicate High IT Investment Potential

1. **Open developer roles** — 5+ concurrent postings signals capacity need
2. **IT budget growth > 15% YoY** — indicates transformation initiative underway
3. **Recent technology acquisitions or platform migrations** — creates integration complexity
4. **Compliance deadlines** — regulatory pressure creates urgency and spending authority
5. **E-commerce revenue growing as a % of total revenue** — indicates digital investment priority
