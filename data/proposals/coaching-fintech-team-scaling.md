# Past Engagement: Engineering Coaching for Scaling Fintech Team

**Domain:** firm-proposal
**Client:** Regional Payment Processing Company (confidential — referenced anonymously)
**Industry:** Financial Services / Fintech
**Service Type:** Coaching and team leading
**Duration:** 10 months
**Delivered:** Q2 2024
**Status:** Completed — follow-on engagement in discussion

## Engagement Summary

Our firm provided embedded engineering coaching and team-leading services to a regional payment processing company ($65M revenue) that had grown its engineering team from 6 to 22 people in 18 months through aggressive hiring. The team was technically capable but lacked delivery process maturity — missing sprint goals 60% of the time, accumulating significant technical debt, and experiencing high junior engineer turnover.

## Problem Statement

The CTO described the situation as "we hired fast, we hired well, but we didn't build the systems and culture to make those people effective." Specifically:
- No consistent engineering practices across teams (code review, testing, deployment)
- Architecture decisions made ad hoc — no architectural review process
- On-call burden falling unevenly on senior engineers, causing burnout
- Junior engineers not receiving structured mentorship and leaving after 6–9 months
- Sprint planning was inconsistent; no reliable way to forecast delivery

## What We Provided

1. **Embedded Technical Lead** — our lead engineer joined as an embedded team lead for the core payments platform team; ran sprint ceremonies, established code review standards, and introduced an architecture decision record (ADR) process
2. **Engineering Manager Coaching** — weekly 1:1 coaching with 3 engineering managers on performance management, team health, and delivery forecasting
3. **Technical Practice Standards** — developed and rolled out shared engineering standards: branching strategy, PR review guidelines, test coverage requirements, and deployment runbooks
4. **On-Call Process Redesign** — built a fair, documented on-call rotation with proper runbooks; reduced mean time to resolution (MTTR) for production incidents by 65%
5. **Junior Engineer Mentorship Program** — designed a structured 6-month onboarding program for new hires; all 4 junior engineers who went through the program are still with the company

## Outcomes

- Sprint goal achievement: 40% → 78% within 6 months
- Junior engineer 12-month retention: 25% → 100% for cohort that went through mentorship program
- Production incident MTTR: reduced 65%
- Technical debt: 3 legacy services decommissioned; test coverage up from 18% to 61%
- CTO: "This engagement changed how we think about engineering culture, not just delivery."

## Technologies Used

Java (Spring Boot), React, PostgreSQL, AWS, GitHub Actions, Jira, PagerDuty

## Pain Points We Solved

- Rapid team growth without delivery process maturity
- High junior engineer attrition
- Inconsistent engineering practices across teams
- On-call burnout on senior engineers
- Missing sprint goals and unpredictable delivery

## Why This Engagement Worked

Coaching engagements succeed when leadership is genuinely committed to change and willing to give the embedded consultant real authority to set standards. The CTO cleared blockers quickly and the coaching relationship was trust-based from week one. We did not build software — we built the capability of the team to build software consistently.
