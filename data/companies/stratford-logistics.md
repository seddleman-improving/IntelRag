# Stratford Logistics Partners

**Domain:** company-profile

**Headquarters:** Chicago, Illinois

## Financial Overview

Stratford Logistics Partners is a third-party logistics (3PL) provider specializing in cold-chain and temperature-controlled freight. Annual revenue was $310 million in 2024, up from $285 million in 2023 and $260 million in 2022. Revenue growth is steady at approximately 9% annually, driven by expansion in pharmaceutical and food & beverage logistics.

IT spending is estimated at 3.6% of revenue — $11.2 million in 2024, up from $9.5 million in 2023 and $8.1 million in 2022. Logistics firms typically spend 2.5–4% of revenue on IT; Stratford is at the upper end, reflecting the complexity of their temperature-controlled tracking systems and the competitive need for real-time visibility tools.

## Software Development Spend Breakdown

Approximately 38% of the IT budget ($4.3 million) goes to software development:
- Transportation Management System (TMS) customization and integration: $1.6 million
- Real-time temperature and cold-chain monitoring platform: $1.1 million
- Customer-facing shipment visibility portal: $0.9 million
- Internal operations tooling and automation: $0.7 million

## IT Headcount and Hiring Signals

Stratford employs 38 IT staff, of whom 15 are developers. Our firm currently has 2 staff-augmented engineers embedded with Stratford's TMS team. Stratford has posted 3 additional developer roles in the past 60 days: 1 Python backend engineer and 2 frontend engineers (React).

## Technology Stack

- **TMS:** McLeod Software (heavily customized)
- **Cloud:** AWS (primary — ECS, RDS, IoT Core for sensor data)
- **Languages:** Python (backend APIs and data processing), React (frontend portals)
- **IoT:** AWS IoT Core for temperature sensor telemetry
- **Data:** PostgreSQL, Redshift (early analytics)

## Current Tech Initiatives

1. **Cold-Chain Telemetry Platform** — building real-time temperature monitoring for pharmaceutical shipments using IoT sensors and AWS IoT Core; Q2 2027 target for full rollout
2. **Customer Visibility Portal v2** — upgrading shipment tracking portal with predictive ETA capabilities and exception alerting; expanding the existing engagement with our firm
3. **TMS API Modernization** — exposing McLeod TMS data via REST APIs to enable customer integrations and replace point-to-point EDI connections
4. **Automated Exception Management** — building an ML-assisted workflow to detect and route logistics exceptions (delays, temperature deviations) without manual dispatcher intervention

## Pain Points

- Cold-chain monitoring platform is behind schedule; original team underestimated the complexity of IoT data processing at scale
- McLeod TMS is a black box to the development team — customization requires expensive vendor professional services for anything beyond surface-level changes
- Customer portal's ETA accuracy is 68% — below the 90% target; predictive model needs significant improvement
- Python backend team (3 engineers) is the bottleneck for all data-intensive work; every initiative is queued behind them
- Data quality from legacy TMS is inconsistent; causing downstream analytics problems
