# Meridian Industrial Solutions

**Domain:** company-profile
**Industry:** Manufacturing
**Headquarters:** Cincinnati, Ohio

## Financial Overview

Meridian Industrial Solutions manufactures precision hydraulic components for heavy equipment and industrial machinery. Annual revenue was $215 million in fiscal year 2024, up from $198 million in 2023 and $183 million in 2022. Growth is steady at approximately 8% per year, driven by expanding into European markets and a new product line for renewable energy equipment.

IT spending is estimated at 2.4% of revenue — $5.2 million in 2024, up from $4.5 million in 2023 and $3.9 million in 2022. The 15% year-over-year budget growth reflects a recently approved digital transformation initiative tied to quality management and supply chain visibility.

## Software Development Spend Breakdown

Approximately 30% of the IT budget ($1.6 million) is allocated to software development:
- Quality management system (QMS) modernization: $0.7 million
- ERP integration and reporting customization: $0.4 million
- Supplier and customer portal development: $0.3 million
- Internal tooling and automation scripts: $0.2 million

## IT Headcount and Hiring Signals

Meridian employs 19 IT staff, of whom 6 are developers. The company recently posted 3 developer roles: 1 full-stack developer (.NET/React), 1 ERP integration specialist (SAP), and 1 QA engineer. The SAP integration role has been open for 3 months, indicating a specialist skill gap similar to Apex Manufacturing.

## Technology Stack

- **ERP:** SAP S/4HANA (recently upgraded from SAP ECC in 2023)
- **QMS:** ETQ Reliance (legacy configuration, needs modernization)
- **Cloud:** Azure (Microsoft 365, some workloads migrated)
- **Languages:** .NET / C#, some Python for reporting automation

## Current Tech Initiatives

1. **QMS Modernization** — replacing manual quality inspection records with a digital QMS integrated with SAP S/4HANA; FDA-adjacent compliance driver for their medical equipment customers
2. **Supplier Portal** — building a supplier collaboration portal for purchase orders, delivery confirmations, and quality certifications; currently managed via email and spreadsheets
3. **SAP Reporting Modernization** — replacing Crystal Reports with a modern analytics layer (Power BI) connected to SAP
4. **Shop Floor Digitization** — tablet-based work instructions and quality checks replacing paper-based processes on the production floor

## Pain Points

- SAP S/4HANA upgrade completed but integrations to QMS and supplier systems are broken or non-existent
- Quality data is trapped in paper records and ETQ — no connection to SAP for traceability
- SAP integration developer role open 3 months without a viable candidate
- Small IT team (6 developers) cannot simultaneously maintain existing systems and build new capabilities
- European expansion creating new compliance requirements (ISO certifications, customs documentation) that need system support
