# Apex Manufacturing Inc.

**Domain:** company-profile
**Industry:** Manufacturing
**Headquarters:** Columbus, Ohio
**Employees:** 3,200
**Status:** Prospect — not a current client

## Financial Overview

Apex Manufacturing reported annual revenue of $480 million in fiscal year 2024, up from $445 million in 2023 and $410 million in 2022. The company operates 6 production facilities across the Midwest and sells industrial components to the automotive and aerospace sectors.

IT spending is estimated at approximately 2.8% of revenue, placing total IT budget around $13.4 million annually in 2024. This is up from an estimated $11.5 million in 2023 and $9.8 million in 2022, representing consistent year-over-year IT investment growth of roughly 17%. This growth rate is above the manufacturing sector average of 9–11% and signals an active digital transformation agenda.

## Software Development Spend Breakdown

Of the $13.4 million IT budget, an estimated 35% ($4.7 million) is allocated to software development and custom application work. This breaks down as follows:
- Legacy ERP modernization and integration: $2.1 million
- Custom shop floor reporting and MES (Manufacturing Execution System) tooling: $1.2 million
- API development for supplier and customer integrations: $0.9 million
- Internal developer tooling and DevOps infrastructure: $0.5 million

The remaining 65% covers infrastructure (on-premise and AWS), licensed software (SAP, Microsoft 365), and IT operations.

## IT Headcount and Hiring Signals

Apex employs approximately 42 IT staff, of whom 14 are software developers or engineers. The company has posted 6 software engineering roles on LinkedIn and Indeed in the past 90 days, including 2 senior .NET developers and 1 integration architect. This hiring pattern is consistent with a team that is struggling to staff up for their modernization roadmap.

## Technology Stack

- **ERP:** SAP S/4HANA (mid-migration from SAP ECC, currently 60% complete)
- **Cloud:** AWS (EC2, RDS, S3) — hybrid with on-premise data center
- **Languages:** .NET / C#, Python (analytics), SQL
- **Integration:** MuleSoft (limited implementation, underutilized)
- **DevOps:** Azure DevOps, early CI/CD adoption

## Current Tech Initiatives

1. **SAP S/4HANA Migration** — largest initiative; targeting completion Q3 2026; behind schedule by approximately 2 quarters
2. **Supplier Portal Rebuild** — replacing a 12-year-old supplier onboarding and order portal with a modern web application; budget estimated at $1.8 million over 18 months
3. **Shop Floor IoT Integration** — connecting CNC machines and production lines to a central analytics dashboard; early proof-of-concept phase
4. **API-first Integration Layer** — building standardized APIs between SAP, their MES system, and customer EDI feeds

## Pain Points

- SAP migration is behind schedule and over budget; internal team lacks ERP integration expertise
- High developer attrition (3 senior engineers left in the past 12 months)
- Shop floor analytics are manual and Excel-based; leadership wants real-time visibility
- MuleSoft investment is underperforming — not enough internal expertise to operationalize it
- Software project delivery has been inconsistent; no formal agile process in place
