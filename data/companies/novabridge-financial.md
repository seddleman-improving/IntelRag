## Financial Overview

NovaBridge Financial is a regional bank and wealth management firm with $2.1 billion in assets under management. Annual revenue was $94 million in 2024, up from $88 million in 2023 and $81 million in 2022. Revenue growth is driven by expansion into digital wealth management products targeting younger high-net-worth clients.

IT spending is estimated at 7.2% of revenue — $6.8 million in 2024, up from $5.9 million in 2023 and $5.1 million in 2022. Financial services firms typically spend 6–9% of revenue on IT, placing NovaBridge at the mid-range. The elevated growth rate (18% year-over-year) reflects significant investment in digital product development and regulatory compliance tooling.

## Software Development Spend Breakdown

An estimated 45% of the IT budget ($3.1 million) goes to software development:
- Digital banking platform enhancements (mobile app, web portal): $1.3 million
- Regulatory and compliance automation (reporting, audit trails): $0.8 million
- Data pipeline and analytics platform: $0.6 million
- Core banking API layer: $0.4 million

## IT Headcount and Hiring Signals

NovaBridge employs 28 IT staff, of whom 11 are developers. The company has posted 4 developer roles in the past 90 days: 2 full-stack engineers (React/Node), 1 data engineer, and 1 platform engineer. The data engineer and platform engineer roles have been open for over 60 days, indicating difficulty filling specialist positions.

## Technology Stack

- **Core Banking:** Fiserv DNA
- **Cloud:** Azure (primary), some AWS for data workloads
- **Languages:** Java (backend services), TypeScript/React (frontend), Python (data)
- **Data Platform:** Azure Data Factory, Databricks (early stage)
- **Mobile:** React Native

## Current Tech Initiatives

1. **Digital Wealth Portal** — building a self-service investment and wealth management portal for clients; 12-month roadmap; currently in design phase
2. **Regulatory Reporting Automation** — replacing manual regulatory report generation with automated pipelines; mandated by compliance team following a 2023 audit finding
3. **Data Platform Build-out** — centralizing data from core banking, CRM, and marketing into Azure Databricks for analytics and AI-ready data layer
4. **API Gateway** — exposing core banking capabilities via REST APIs to enable third-party fintech integrations and their own mobile app

## Pain Points

- Regulatory reporting is manual and error-prone; created audit finding in 2023
- Digital product roadmap is ambitious but engineering team is too small to execute at required pace
- Data is siloed across Fiserv DNA, CRM, and Excel — no unified analytics capability
- Competitive pressure from fintech disruptors (Robinhood, Betterment) eating into younger client acquisition
- Two specialist engineering roles (data and platform) have been open more than 60 days
