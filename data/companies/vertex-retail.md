# Vertex Retail Group

**Domain:** company-profile
**Industry:** Retail
**Headquarters:** Dallas, Texas
**Employees:** 6,800 (including 4,200 store associates)
**Status:** Prospect — not a current client

## Financial Overview

Vertex Retail Group operates 210 specialty home goods retail stores across the South and Midwest. Annual revenue was $780 million in 2024, up from $720 million in 2023 and $640 million in 2022. Revenue growth has been driven by e-commerce expansion — online sales grew from 12% to 22% of total revenue over this period.

IT spending is estimated at 2.1% of revenue — $16.4 million in 2024, up from $12.2 million in 2023 and $9.6 million in 2022. The rapid IT budget growth (35% year-over-year in 2024) is the highest of any company in our target set and directly tracks the e-commerce expansion initiative. Retailers typically spend 1.5–3% of revenue on IT; Vertex is moving to the upper range as digital commerce becomes central to their business model.

## Software Development Spend Breakdown

An estimated 40% of the IT budget ($6.6 million) is allocated to software development:
- E-commerce platform development (custom features on Shopify Plus): $2.2 million
- Order management system (OMS) customization and integration: $1.4 million
- Mobile app (iOS/Android) for loyalty program: $1.1 million
- In-store POS system modernization: $0.9 million
- Data and personalization platform: $1.0 million

## IT Headcount and Hiring Signals

Vertex employs 58 IT staff, of whom 24 are software engineers. The company has posted 9 developer roles in the past 90 days — the most active hiring of any company in our prospect list. Roles include: 3 full-stack engineers, 2 mobile developers, 2 backend engineers for OMS integration, 1 data engineer, and 1 engineering manager. The sheer volume of open roles relative to team size suggests the team is scaling rapidly and stretched thin.

## Technology Stack

- **E-commerce:** Shopify Plus (primary), custom React storefront
- **OMS:** Manhattan Associates (partially customized)
- **Cloud:** AWS (primary), Cloudflare for CDN and edge
- **Languages:** TypeScript/React (frontend), Node.js (backend services), Python (data)
- **Mobile:** React Native
- **Data:** Snowflake, dbt, Looker

## Current Tech Initiatives

1. **E-commerce Platform Expansion** — adding B2B wholesale portal to existing B2C Shopify Plus platform; significant custom development required
2. **OMS Modernization** — replatforming the order management system to support omnichannel fulfillment (buy online, pick up in store, ship from store)
3. **Loyalty Mobile App v2** — rebuilding the iOS/Android loyalty app with personalization features driven by purchase history
4. **Real-time Personalization Engine** — building a recommendation and personalization layer using Snowflake and a Python ML pipeline
5. **POS Modernization** — replacing 10-year-old POS terminals with cloud-connected iPads running a custom React Native app

## Pain Points

- Engineering team is growing too fast — 9 open roles, onboarding strain, inconsistent code quality across new hires
- OMS integration is blocking e-commerce roadmap — order routing and inventory sync are fragile and causing customer-visible issues
- Mobile app has 2.1-star rating on App Store due to performance issues and bugs — executive priority to fix before holiday season
- No formal engineering processes; teams are building inconsistently as the company scales
- First engineering manager hire just made — they need structured delivery processes from scratch
